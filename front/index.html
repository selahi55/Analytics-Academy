Who are we?

The New Generation United Nations (NGUN) is a dynamic initiative by Global Human Rights Defence (GHRD), a non-profit organisation based in The Hague, Netherlands. With consultative status at the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) since 2015, GHRD is committed to empowering the next generation of leaders.

Through NGUN, the organization seeks to amplify the voices of young people, ensuring their perspectives are not only heard but actively integrated into discussions and decision-making processes at the United Nations (UN) level. This initiative reflects GHRD's broader mission to advocate for human rights for minority and marginalised groups and to foster global dialogue.

This initiative will culminate in a proposal presentation at the upcoming UN Human Rights Council session.

Our event :

The "New Generation Uniting Nations" program will take place on May 14th and 16th, 2025, at the Peace Palace and on May 15th at Amare in The Hague. The event will feature keynote addresses, introductory lectures, and roundtable discussions on contemporary issues, focusing on empowering marginalised voices.

  • May 14th - Welcome, registration, and opening ceremony
  • May 15th - Roundtables and networking event
  • May 16th - Closing ceremony

Activities

What to expect?

  • Round tables: 3 days of roundtables and panel discussions on pressing youth-related issues.
  • Policy engagement: Development of a proposal for the UN Human Rights Council.
  • Group activities: Group discussions to prepare recommendations or solutions.
  • Journalism Contest: Write articles with the chance to be published and join GHRD for 2 months.

Purpose of collaboration

  • To inspire, equip, and empower youth leaders to address human rights challenges.
  • To elevate the voices of marginalized youth in global discussions.
  • To offer young people opportunities to learn, exchange ideas, and network.
  • To provide a space for collective action and advocacy.
  • To foster youth networks and ongoing engagement on human rights issues.
  • To increase visibility for youth-led initiatives worldwide.

Benefits of partnership

  • Networking: Build a strong network of organizations for collaboration.
  • Advocacy: Influence global policymaking on youth participation and rights.
  • Unlock Youth Potential: Engage directly with youth to foster skills and leadership.
  • Capacity Building: Support youth advocacy through workshops and training.

What we are seeking?

  • Financial Cooperation: Support participant funding for accommodation and travel.
  • Become a Speaker: Represent your organization at our events.
  • Become a Trainer: Lead training sessions for youth empowerment.
  • Promoting: Help spread awareness through media platforms.
