Who are we?

The New Generation United Nations (NGUN) is a dynamic initiative by Global Human Rights Defence (GHRD), a non-profit organisation based in The Hague, Netherlands. With consultative status at the United Nations Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) since 2015, GHRD is committed to empowering the next generation of leaders. Through NGUN, the organization seeks to amplify the voices of young people, ensuring their perspectives are not only heard but actively integrated into discussions and decision-making processes at the United Nations (UN) level. This initiative reflects GHRD's broader mission to advocate for human rights for minority and marginalised groups and to foster global dialogue.

This initiative, led by GHRD, will furthermore culminate in a proposal presentation at the upcoming UN Human Rights Council session

Our event :

The "New Generation Uniting Nations" program will take place on May 14th and 16th, 2025, at the Peace Palace and on May 15th at Amare in The Hague, the City of Peace and Justice, a venue of profound global significance in promoting human rights. The event will feature keynote addresses, introductory lectures, and roundtable discussions on crucial contemporary issues, focusing on empowering marginalised voices.


  • May 14th - Welcome, registration, and opening ceremony
  • May 15th - Roundtables and networking event
  • May 16th - Closing ceremony

Activities

What to expect?

  • Round tables: 3 days of roundtables and panel discussions on pressing youth-related issues.
  • Policy engagement: Development of a proposal to present at the next session of the UN Human Rights Council.
  • Group activities: At the end of every roundtable, the participants will be divided into groups of 6-7 people. The discussion within small groups aims at preparing a recommendation on a specific issue or developing a project as a proposed solution.
  • Journalism Contest: Write an article focused on a topic explored during the event. The winning group’s article will be published on our website, and the group will be given a 2-month opportunity to write for GHRD.

Purpose of collaboration

  • To , equip, and empower the next generation of leaders to address ongoing human rights challenges.
  • To elevate the voices of young people, especially those from marginalized communities, ensuring their perspectives are not only heard but actively integrated into discussions and decision-making processes within the international human rights framework and global policy.
  • To offer young people opportunities to learn, exchange ideas and network with global advocates, practitioners, and experts.
  • To provide a space for collective action, advocacy, and mobilization on key human rights issues such as climate change, gender equality, and migration.
  • Foster the development of youth networks and communities of practice that continue to collaborate on human rights issues, ensuring sustained engagement beyond the scope of the project.
  • Increase visibility for youth-led human rights initiatives by leveraging the reach and influence of partner organizations to amplify the work of young leaders and attract global support for their causes.

Benefits of partnership

  • Networking: Build the foundations for future partnerships and collaborations by establishing a solid network of organisations with the same goals.
  • Advocacy: Engage in meaningful opportunities to influence global policy-making concerning youth participation and rights. Institutions will have the chance to actively contribute to the preparation of statements and recommendations for the United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) sessions, ensuring that youth perspectives are prominently featured in international discussions.
  • Unlock Youth Potential: This partnership will create exciting opportunities to engage directly with young people, helping to foster their skills and confidence. By facilitating discussions on critical issues that matter to youth, such as climate change, economic crises, and governance, we can empower the next generation to share their perspectives and drive positive change.
  • Capacity Building: GHRD’s expertise in human rights advocacy and youth engagement can help institutions develop their capacities through training and workshops, improving their ability to address youth-related issues.
  • Joint Research Initiatives: Collaborate to produce research papers focusing on critical youth issues such as climate change and economic rights crises, barriers to participation in decision-making and governance towards an ambiguous future, and the erosion of fundamental rights through the rise of authoritarianism, populism, and illiberalism.
  • Organising Events, Webinars, and Trainings: Establishing a long-term partnership will create valuable opportunities to organise a series of events, webinars, and training sessions for youth. These initiatives will not only enhance skills and knowledge but also foster a supportive community that encourages ongoing dialogue and collaboration on pressing issues.

What we are seeking?

  • Financial Cooperation: A key objective of this partnership is to create a funding pool to support participants from minority and marginalized groups, enabling them to attend by covering their accommodation and/or travel costs.
  • Become a Speaker: Through this partnership, we are seeking to give you the opportunity to represent your organization by becoming one of our speakers at the roundtables and/or the opening and/or closing ceremony.
  • Become a Trainer: On the first day, we are seeking to organize training sessions for youth, focusing on how to engage effectively on the international and European stage, how to prepare policy and recommendation papers, and how to enhance their leadership and public speaking skills.
  • Promoting: Promotion is crucial to the success of this event. Through our combined social media platforms, we are seeking to effectively promote the event and reach young people.
  • Be part of the Networking Event: At the end of the second day, there will be a networking event where you can set up a stand to promote your organization, engage with youth, and establish meaningful connections.
